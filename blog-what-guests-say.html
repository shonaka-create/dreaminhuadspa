Guest Experiences

“I didn't know my scalp could feel like this”

Dreamin' Team Guest Experiences 3 min read

Five stars from more than 140 guests. But the number isn't the interesting part — what people choose to write about is.

When we read back through our Google reviews, the same few things come up again and again. Not the décor. Not the products. Three specific experiences, described by people who had never had a Japanese wet head spa before and did not quite know what to expect.

1. “I fell asleep — and I never fall asleep”

This is the one we hear most. Guests arrive intending to relax, and instead they properly, genuinely go under.

There is a mechanical reason for it. Our shampoo stand is completely flat, imported from Japan, so your neck is never bent back over a basin rim. Your body has nothing to brace against — and once the bracing stops, ninety minutes of warm water and layered massage does the rest.

“The name Dreamin' Head Spa exists for a reason. It genuinely felt like dreaming.” Paraphrased from a Google review — 5 stars

2. “I learned more about my scalp in one session than in my whole life”

The second thing guests remark on is that they leave knowing something.

Every visit starts with a consultation and a trichology-informed scalp diagnosis. Takashi will tell you what he can see — whether your scalp is oily or dehydrated, tight or inflamed, whether the shampoo you have been loyal to for years is actually working against you — and then choose the products and technique for what is actually in front of him, rather than running a fixed routine.

“I've learnt more about my hair and scalp condition than I ever knew. Regular salons don't interest me any more.” Paraphrased from a Google review — 5 stars

Several guests have told us they have quietly stopped going to ordinary salons since. We take that as the compliment it is.

3. “Unhurried, and unmistakably a craft”

The third theme is about pace. There are no shortened express menus here — the shortest head spa is ninety minutes, and nobody is watching the clock or turning the room over.

Guests notice that. They describe the treatment as calm, deliberate, precise; and they notice that the cut and blow-dry at the end are done with the same care as the spa itself. More than once, someone has come to us to rescue an uneven cut from elsewhere and left saying the result was better than what they originally asked for.

“Not rushed, not generic — just skill and care. My scalp, neck and shoulders felt completely reset.” Paraphrased from a Google review — 5 stars

What surprises first-timers

Two things, usually.

  • You don't leave with wet hair. The blow-dry is part of every head spa, never an extra charge. You walk out styled and ready for the rest of your day.
  • It is not a hair wash. A wash cleans hair. A head spa treats the skin your hair grows from — lifting away build-up, then feeding nutrients and moisture back in. The difference is obvious the moment you feel it, and difficult to describe until then.

Which is really the point of this article. We can keep writing about it, but ninety minutes in the chair will explain it better than we can.
