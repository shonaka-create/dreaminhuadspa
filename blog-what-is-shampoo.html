What is shampoo?

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

Most of us use shampoo and conditioner regularly, but have you ever stopped to ask what they actually do?

Many people think shampoo and conditioner have the same purpose, or that conditioner is optional.

In reality, they perform completely different roles, and understanding the difference is essential for maintaining healthy hair.

At Dreamin’, we believe that knowing why you use each product is just as important as choosing the right one.

Let's explore what shampoo and conditioner actually do and why both play an important role in a healthy hair care routine.

What Is Shampoo?

Shampoo is a cleansing product designed primarily to clean your scalp.

Throughout the day, your scalp naturally collects:

  • Sebum (natural oil)
  • Sweat
  • Dead skin cells
  • Dust and pollution
  • Styling products
  • Product residue

If these impurities aren't removed regularly, they can build up and affect the overall health of your scalp.

The primary purpose of shampoo is to gently remove this buildup while maintaining your scalp's natural balance.

Healthy hair always begins with a clean, healthy scalp.

Should You Shampoo Your Hair or Your Scalp?

One of the biggest misconceptions is that shampoo is for washing your hair.

In reality, shampoo should be focused on the scalp.

As you massage the shampoo into your scalp, the lather naturally cleanses the lengths of your hair as it rinses through.

Scrubbing the mid-lengths and ends aggressively is usually unnecessary and may contribute to dryness, especially if your hair is coloured or chemically treated.

What Does Conditioner Do?

Unlike shampoo, conditioner is designed to care for the hair—not the scalp.

During shampooing, the hair cuticle lifts slightly as oil and impurities are removed.

Conditioner helps by:

  • Smoothing the cuticle
  • Replenishing moisture
  • Reducing friction
  • Improving softness
  • Increasing shine
  • Making hair easier to detangle
  • Helping reduce breakage

It protects the hair after cleansing rather than cleaning it.

Do You Really Need Conditioner?

In most cases, yes.

Shampoo cleanses, but it also removes some of the natural oils that help protect your hair.

Conditioner helps replace lost moisture and leaves the hair smoother and more manageable.

Without conditioner, hair may become:

  • Dry
  • Tangled
  • Frizzy
  • More prone to breakage
  • Dull in appearance

The longer or more chemically treated your hair is, the more important conditioning becomes.

Should You Apply Conditioner to Your Scalp?

Usually, no.

Unless a conditioner has been specifically formulated for scalp application, it should generally be applied from the mid-lengths to the ends of the hair.

Applying a standard conditioner directly to the scalp may contribute to:

  • Excess oil
  • Product buildup
  • Hair feeling heavy
  • Reduced volume

Your scalp and your hair have different needs, which is why shampoo and conditioner are applied to different areas.

Shampoo vs. Conditioner

ShampooConditioner
Cleanses the scalpNourishes the hair
Removes oil and buildupRestores moisture
Creates a clean scalp environmentSmooths and protects the hair

Focus on the scalpFocus on mid-lengths and ends

Both products work together to maintain healthy hair.

Can You Skip Conditioner?

Some people with very short hair may choose not to use conditioner.

However, for most people—especially those with:

  • Medium to long hair
  • Colour-treated hair
  • Bleached hair
  • Curly hair
  • Dry hair
  • Heat-styled hair

Conditioner is an essential part of protecting the hair from everyday damage.

Choosing the Right Products Matters

Not every shampoo or conditioner is suitable for every scalp and hair type.

For the best results, choose products based on your individual needs, such as:

  • Oily scalp
  • Dry scalp
  • Sensitive scalp
  • Damaged hair
  • Colour-treated hair
  • Fine hair
  • Curly hair

The right routine is always personalised.
