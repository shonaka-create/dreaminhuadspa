Scalp & Hair Health

How heat damages your hair

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

Blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners are part of many people's daily routine.

While these styling tools can help create beautiful hairstyles, repeated exposure to high temperatures can gradually weaken your hair—even if the damage isn't immediately visible.

At Dreamin’, we believe that understanding how heat affects your hair is the first step toward preventing unnecessary damage and maintaining healthy, beautiful hair.

Let's explore what heat really does to your hair and whether damaged hair can recover.

How Does Heat Damage Hair?

Hair is made primarily of keratin, a strong protein that gives each strand its structure and elasticity.

When excessive heat is applied repeatedly, several things happen:

  • Moisture inside the hair evaporates.
  • The protective cuticle begins to lift and crack.
  • Keratin proteins become weakened.
  • Hair loses flexibility and strength.

Over time, the hair becomes increasingly dry, fragile, and more likely to break.

Signs of Heat Damage

Heat damage often develops gradually.

Common signs include:

  • Dry, rough hair
  • Split ends
  • Increased breakage
  • Frizz
  • Dull appearance
  • Hair tangles easily
  • Loss of elasticity
  • Hair feels stiff or brittle

If your hair no longer feels soft after washing or struggles to hold moisture, heat damage may be one of the causes.

What Happens If You Ignore Heat Damage?

Many people continue styling damaged hair every day without changing their routine.

Unfortunately, heat damage usually becomes worse over time.

Without proper care, you may notice:

  • More split ends
  • Hair snapping during brushing
  • Thinner-looking ends
  • Increased frizz
  • Reduced shine
  • Difficulty growing longer hair

Once the protective cuticle is severely damaged, the hair fibre becomes much more vulnerable to everyday wear and tear.

Can Heat-Damaged Hair Repair Itself?

The simple answer is no.

Hair is made of dead keratin cells, which means it cannot regenerate once damaged.

However, that doesn't mean you can't improve its condition.

Repair-focused hair care can:

  • Improve hydration
  • Smooth the cuticle
  • Reduce breakage
  • Improve softness
  • Help protect against future damage

These treatments improve the appearance and manageability of damaged hair, even though they cannot completely reverse existing structural damage.

Should You Cut Heat-Damaged Hair?

It depends on the severity of the damage.

If the ends are:

  • Splitting
  • Snapping easily
  • Extremely dry
  • Difficult to manage

A trim is often the healthiest option.

Removing damaged ends prevents split ends from travelling further up the hair shaft and helps your hair look healthier overall.

If the damage is mild, consistent repair treatments and reducing heat exposure may significantly improve your hair's appearance while allowing you to maintain your length.

How to Prevent Heat Damage

Healthy styling habits can make a significant difference.

Lower the Temperature

Most hair doesn't require the highest heat setting. Using the lowest effective temperature helps minimise unnecessary damage.

Avoid Daily Heat Styling

Giving your hair regular breaks from heat styling helps preserve moisture and reduces long-term damage.

Never Use Hot Tools on Wet Hair

Hair is at its weakest when wet. Using a straightener or curling iron on damp hair can cause severe structural damage.

Always ensure your hair is completely dry before using heated styling tools.

Always Use a Heat Protectant

A heat protectant forms a protective barrier around the hair shaft, helping reduce moisture loss, minimise friction, and protect the cuticle from high temperatures.

Using a heat protectant before every styling session is one of the easiest ways to reduce heat damage.

Healthy Hair Starts with a Healthy Scalp

Although heat primarily damages the hair shaft, healthy hair always begins with a healthy scalp.

Supporting scalp health encourages stronger new hair growth, while protecting your existing hair helps maintain its strength, softness, and shine.

Healthy hair is built from both the roots and the ends.
