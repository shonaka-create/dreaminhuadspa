Scalp & Hair Health

Why does hair split and break?

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

Have you ever noticed tiny white dots on your hair, split ends at the tips, or strands that seem to snap easily when brushing?

These are all signs that your hair's structure has been damaged.

At Dreamin’, we believe that understanding why hair breaks and how split ends develop is the first step toward maintaining stronger, healthier hair.

Let's explore what happens inside the hair fibre and how you can protect it from further damage.

Understanding Your Hair Structure

Each strand of hair is made primarily of a protein called keratin and consists of three layers:

  • Cuticle – The outer protective layer made of overlapping scales.
  • Cortex – The middle layer that gives hair its strength, elasticity, and natural colour.
  • Medulla – The innermost core (not present in every hair type).

When the cuticle is smooth and intact, it protects the cortex from moisture loss and external damage.

However, once the cuticle becomes worn away, the inner structure becomes exposed and much more fragile.

Why Does Hair Break?

Hair doesn't suddenly snap without a reason.

Breakage usually occurs after the hair has gradually become weaker over time.

Common causes include:

  • Hair colouring and bleaching
  • Perms and chemical straightening
  • Frequent heat styling
  • UV exposure
  • Hard water and mineral buildup
  • Aggressive brushing
  • Tight hairstyles
  • Lack of moisture
  • Protein loss

As damage accumulates, the hair loses elasticity.

Instead of stretching slightly and returning to its original shape, the strand eventually snaps under tension.

What Are Split Ends?

Split ends occur when the protective cuticle has been damaged so extensively that the inner fibres begin separating.

Instead of one smooth strand, the hair starts splitting into multiple fibres.

This usually begins at the ends of the hair, which are the oldest and have experienced the most environmental and mechanical stress.

Why Are Split Ends a Problem?

Many people think split ends are only a cosmetic issue, but they can lead to much more damage.

Once a strand begins splitting, the damage can continue travelling further up the hair shaft.

This may result in:

  • More breakage
  • Increased frizz
  • Tangles
  • Rough texture
  • Loss of shine
  • Hair appearing thinner
  • Difficulty growing longer hair

The longer split ends are left untreated, the more fragile the hair becomes.

Can Split Ends Be Repaired?

This is one of the most common questions we hear.

The answer is not permanently.

Because hair is made of dead keratin cells, split ends cannot naturally heal or grow back together.

The only permanent way to remove split ends is by trimming them.

However, modern hair care products can temporarily seal and smooth split ends, helping hair look healthier while protecting it from further damage.

How to Prevent Hair Breakage and Split Ends

Healthy hair requires consistent care.

Keep Hair Hydrated

Dry hair is more likely to become brittle and break.

Regular conditioning and deep hydration help improve flexibility and softness.

Reduce Heat Damage

Always use a heat protectant and avoid excessive temperatures when styling.

Handle Hair Gently

Avoid aggressive brushing, rough towel drying, and tight hairstyles that place unnecessary stress on the hair.

Trim Hair Regularly

Regular trims prevent split ends from travelling further up the hair shaft.

Even small trims every few months can make a noticeable difference.

Healthy Hair Starts with a Healthy Scalp

Although breakage and split ends occur on the hair shaft, healthy hair begins beneath the surface.

A healthy scalp provides the ideal environment for producing stronger, healthier hair from the very beginning.

Taking care of your scalp today helps support healthier hair tomorrow.
