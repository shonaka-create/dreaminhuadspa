Why pre-rinsing matters

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 5 min read

When washing your hair, many people immediately reach for the shampoo.

However, one of the most important steps in a healthy hair care routine actually comes before the shampoo.

It's called pre-rinsing—thoroughly rinsing your hair and scalp with warm water before applying shampoo.

At Dreamin’, we believe this simple step can make a significant difference to your scalp health, shampoo performance, and overall hair condition.

Let's explore why pre-rinsing matters and how to do it correctly.

What Is Pre-Rinsing?

Pre-rinsing is the process of thoroughly soaking your hair and scalp with warm water before using shampoo.

Rather than a quick splash of water, it involves allowing warm water to flow through your scalp and hair for one to three minutes, gently loosening oil, sweat, dead skin cells, and surface dirt.

Many professional Japanese Head Spa treatments begin with this step because it prepares the scalp for a more effective cleanse.

Why Is Pre-Rinsing So Important?

A proper pre-rinse does much more than simply wet your hair.

It helps to:

  • Remove sweat and surface dirt
  • Loosen excess sebum (natural oil)
  • Wash away dust and environmental pollutants
  • Reduce product buildup
  • Prepare the scalp for shampoo
  • Allow shampoo to spread more evenly

In many cases, warm water alone can remove a surprising amount of the day's buildup before shampoo is even applied.

Did You Know?

Many hair professionals estimate that a large percentage of surface dirt and sweat can be removed with warm water alone, allowing shampoo to focus on cleansing the remaining oil and impurities rather than working through heavy buildup.

This helps improve both cleansing performance and scalp comfort.

Benefits of Pre-Rinsing

Taking an extra minute before shampooing offers several benefits.

Better Shampoo Performance

When the scalp is already wet and surface debris has been loosened, shampoo lathers more easily and cleanses more effectively.

Healthier Scalp

Removing excess oil and debris before shampooing helps create a cleaner scalp environment and reduces unnecessary buildup.

Less Shampoo Required

Because your hair is already clean of surface impurities, you may need less shampoo to achieve a rich lather.

Reduced Friction

Properly saturated hair creates less friction during washing, helping minimise unnecessary damage to the hair cuticle.

How Long Should You Pre-Rinse?

For most people, one to three minutes of warm water is ideal.

If you have:

  • Long hair
  • Thick hair
  • Oily scalp
  • Heavy styling products

you may benefit from rinsing for a little longer.

The goal is to completely saturate both the scalp and hair before applying shampoo.

Should the Water Be Hot?

No.

Very hot water can strip away too much natural oil and may irritate the scalp.

Instead, use comfortably warm water.

Warm water helps dissolve excess oil while remaining gentle on both the scalp and hair.

What Happens If You Skip This Step?

Skipping a proper pre-rinse may result in:

  • Shampoo not lathering well
  • Using more shampoo than necessary
  • Product buildup remaining on the scalp
  • Less effective cleansing
  • An oily scalp shortly after washing

Over time, inadequate cleansing may contribute to scalp discomfort and buildup.

Healthy Hair Starts with a Healthy Scalp

A healthy scalp doesn't depend on expensive products alone.

Simple techniques, like taking the time to pre-rinse properly, can make a noticeable difference to your overall scalp health.

When your scalp is clean and balanced, your hair has the best opportunity to grow strong, healthy, and beautiful.

Small Habits Make a Big Difference

Healthy hair doesn't start when you apply conditioner—it starts before the shampoo even touches your scalp.

By taking just a few extra minutes to thoroughly rinse your hair before shampooing, you can improve cleansing, support scalp health, and get better results from every wash.

At Dreamin’, we believe healthy hair is built through education, professional techniques, and consistent home care.

Because sometimes, the smallest changes create the biggest improvements.
