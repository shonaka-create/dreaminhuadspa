What hair care products should you use?

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

Choosing hair care products today can feel overwhelming. With countless brands promising healthier, shinier, and stronger hair, it can be difficult to know which products are truly worth investing in.

At Dreamin’, we believe that great hair care isn't about following trends—it's about choosing products that support both your scalp and your hair.

Just as importantly, we believe you should feel confident in the products you use every day.

Let's explore what to look for when choosing hair care products and why we confidently recommend MONAT.

Healthy Hair Starts with a Healthy Scalp

Many people focus only on repairing damaged hair, but healthy hair begins beneath the surface.

Your scalp is where every strand of hair grows. If your scalp isn't healthy, your hair won't have the best opportunity to grow strong and beautiful.

A quality hair care routine should support:

  • A clean, balanced scalp
  • Healthy hair growth
  • Hydrated, stronger hair
  • Long-term hair health

Looking after your scalp is just as important as looking after your hair.

What Should You Look for in Hair Care Products?

Not all hair care products are created equally.

When choosing products, consider whether they:

  • Support both the scalp and hair
  • Cleanse gently without over-drying
  • Help maintain moisture balance
  • Strengthen damaged hair
  • Match your individual hair and scalp needs
  • Clearly list their ingredients

Choosing products based only on fragrance, packaging, or popularity doesn't always lead to healthier hair.

Why Ingredients Matter

Your scalp absorbs everything you apply to it.

That's why understanding a product's ingredients is so important.

High-quality formulations should help:

  • Support the scalp's natural barrier
  • Maintain hydration
  • Improve hair strength
  • Reduce breakage
  • Protect against daily environmental stress

Knowing exactly what you're using gives you greater confidence in your daily hair care routine.

Every Scalp Is Different

No two scalps are exactly the same.

Some people struggle with:

  • Oily scalp
  • Dry scalp
  • Sensitive scalp
  • Dandruff
  • Product buildup
  • Colour-treated hair
  • Damaged hair
  • Fine or thinning hair

The best routine is always the one that's tailored to your individual scalp and hair condition.
