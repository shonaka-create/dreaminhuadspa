What is a hair serum?

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 5 min read

Hair serums have become one of the most popular hair care products on the market.

Some promise smoother hair, others claim to repair damage, reduce frizz, or add shine.

But what exactly is a hair serum, and is it really necessary?

At Dreamin’, we believe that every product should have a purpose. Understanding what a hair serum does—and what it doesn't do—will help you decide whether it's the right addition to your hair care routine.

Let's take a closer look.

What Is a Hair Serum?

A hair serum is a leave-in product designed to protect the outside of the hair while improving its appearance and manageability.

Unlike shampoo or conditioner, a serum remains on the hair after styling.

Most hair serums create a lightweight protective layer around the hair shaft that helps defend against everyday damage.

What Does a Hair Serum Do?

A quality hair serum can provide several benefits, including:

  • Smoothing the hair cuticle
  • Reducing frizz and flyaways
  • Adding shine
  • Improving softness
  • Protecting against humidity
  • Reducing friction during brushing and styling
  • Helping protect hair from environmental stress

Many serums also make hair easier to style and leave it looking healthier without feeling heavy.

Can a Hair Serum Repair Damaged Hair?

This is one of the biggest misconceptions.

A hair serum cannot permanently repair damaged hair.

Hair is made of dead keratin cells, so once it has been damaged, it cannot heal itself.

However, a serum can:

  • Improve the appearance of damaged hair
  • Smooth rough cuticles
  • Help reduce breakage caused by friction
  • Protect hair from further damage
  • Make hair feel softer and healthier

Think of a hair serum as protection and maintenance, rather than a cure.

Do You Need a Hair Serum?

Not everyone needs one, but many people can benefit from using a hair serum as part of their routine.

A hair serum is especially helpful if you have:

  • Frizzy hair
  • Dry hair
  • Colour-treated hair
  • Heat-styled hair
  • Hair that's difficult to manage
  • Dull-looking hair
  • Long hair prone to tangling

If your hair is already healthy, a serum can help maintain its condition and protect it from daily stress.

When Should You Apply a Hair Serum?

Most hair serums are designed to be applied to:

  • Clean, towel-dried hair before blow-drying, or
  • Dry hair as a finishing product.

Always follow the manufacturer's instructions, as different formulas are designed for different purposes.

Generally, focus on the mid-lengths and ends of the hair, where damage is most common.

Unless a serum is specifically formulated for scalp use, avoid applying it directly to your scalp.

How Is a Hair Serum Different from a Hair Oil?

Although they may seem similar, they serve different purposes.

Hair SerumHair Oil
Forms a protective layer on the hairNourishes and softens the hair
Helps reduce frizzHelps replenish moisture and lipids
Adds shineHelps reduce dryness
Protects against humidity and frictionImproves softness and flexibility
Ideal before or after stylingIdeal for nourishing dry hair

Some products combine the benefits of both, but understanding their primary role helps you choose the right one for your hair.

Healthy Hair Starts with a Healthy Scalp

A hair serum helps protect the hair you already have—but healthy hair always begins at the scalp.

A healthy scalp provides the best environment for growing stronger, healthier hair.

That's why the most effective hair care routine always includes both scalp care and hair protection.
