Scalp & Hair Health

Understanding dry scalp

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

A dry scalp is one of the most common concerns we see at Dreamin’ Head Spa & Sculpture.

Many people notice tightness, itchiness, or small white flakes and immediately assume they have dandruff. However, a dry scalp and dandruff are not always the same thing.

Understanding the cause of your dry scalp is the first step towards choosing the right care.

What Is a Dry Scalp?

A dry scalp occurs when the skin on your scalp lacks sufficient moisture.

Just like the skin on your face or hands, your scalp can become dry, sensitive, and uncomfortable when its natural moisture barrier is disrupted.

Common symptoms include:

  • Tightness after washing
  • Mild itchiness
  • Small, dry white flakes
  • A feeling of dryness around the hairline
  • Hair that feels dull or lacks shine
  • Increased sensitivity to hair products

Unlike oily dandruff, the flakes associated with a dry scalp are usually small, dry, and easy to brush away.

What Causes a Dry Scalp?

There is rarely just one cause.

Several factors may contribute to a dry scalp, including:

Overwashing

Shampooing too frequently—especially with harsh cleansers—may remove too much of the scalp’s natural oil.

Hot Water

Very hot showers can strip moisture from both the scalp and hair.

Warm water is generally a better choice.

Weather

Living on the Gold Coast means your scalp is regularly exposed to strong sunlight, wind, air conditioning, salt water, and swimming pools.

These environmental factors can all contribute to dryness.

Heat Styling

Frequent use of hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons may affect both the hair and the scalp.

Harsh Hair Products

Products containing strong cleansing agents or excessive alcohol may leave some scalps feeling dry or irritated.

Age and Lifestyle

As we age, natural oil production can change.

Stress, poor sleep, dehydration, and nutrition may also influence how comfortable your scalp feels.

Why Dry Scalp Shouldn’t Be Ignored

A dry scalp is more than just an uncomfortable feeling.

If left unmanaged, it may lead to:

  • Ongoing itchiness
  • Increased sensitivity
  • More visible flaking
  • Hair that appears dull
  • Greater discomfort during styling

Constant scratching may also irritate the scalp further and increase the risk of damaging the skin.

What NOT to Do

One of the biggest mistakes people make is trying to scrub the dryness away.

If your scalp feels dry, avoid:

  • Scratching with your fingernails
  • Washing your hair with very hot water
  • Using harsh clarifying shampoos too often
  • Applying large amounts of heavy oils without understanding the cause
  • Ignoring persistent irritation or inflammation

If your scalp is red, painful, weeping, or the flakes are thick and oily, the cause may not simply be dryness. In these situations, it is important to seek advice from your GP or dermatologist.

How to Care for a Dry Scalp

Looking after a dry scalp is about restoring balance rather than simply adding moisture.

We recommend:

  • Choosing a gentle shampoo suitable for your scalp
  • Washing with warm rather than hot water
  • Avoiding excessive heat styling
  • Staying hydrated
  • Protecting your scalp from excessive sun exposure
  • Following a personalised home-care routine

Consistency is often more important than using many different products.
