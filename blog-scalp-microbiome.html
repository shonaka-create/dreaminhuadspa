Scalp & Hair Health

Your scalp microbiome

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

Did you know that millions of microorganisms are living on your scalp right now?

While the word "bacteria" often sounds negative, the truth is that not all bacteria are harmful. In fact, many are essential for maintaining a healthy scalp.

At Dreamin’, we believe that understanding your scalp microbiome is one of the keys to healthier hair and a healthier scalp.

Let's explore what lives on your scalp, why these microorganisms are important, and how keeping them in balance supports long-term scalp health.

What Is the Scalp Microbiome?

The scalp microbiome is the community of naturally occurring microorganisms that live on the surface of your scalp.

These include:

  • Beneficial bacteria
  • Naturally occurring yeast (fungi)
  • Other harmless microorganisms

In a healthy scalp, these microorganisms exist in balance and play an important role in protecting your skin.

The goal isn't to eliminate them—it's to maintain a healthy balance.

Why Are These Microorganisms Important?

A balanced scalp microbiome helps to:

  • Protect the scalp from harmful microorganisms
  • Support the skin's natural barrier
  • Help regulate oil production
  • Reduce irritation
  • Maintain overall scalp health

Just like your gut has a microbiome that supports digestion, your scalp has its own ecosystem that supports healthy skin and healthy-looking hair.

Common Microorganisms Found on the Scalp

Cutibacterium acnes

Despite its name, Cutibacterium acnes isn't always harmful.

On a healthy scalp, it helps maintain the skin's natural environment by interacting with sebum (your scalp's natural oil).

Problems may occur if the balance of microorganisms is disrupted.

Staphylococcus epidermidis

This is one of the most common beneficial bacteria found on healthy skin.

It helps defend the scalp against potentially harmful microorganisms and supports the scalp's protective barrier.

A healthy population of this bacteria is considered a normal part of healthy skin.

Malassezia

Malassezia is a naturally occurring yeast that lives on almost everyone's scalp.

In normal amounts, it causes no problems.

However, if excess oil is present or the scalp becomes unbalanced, Malassezia may multiply excessively.

This can contribute to:

  • Dandruff
  • Itching
  • Flaking
  • Scalp irritation

The goal isn't to remove Malassezia completely—it naturally belongs on the scalp. The goal is to keep it in balance.

What Happens When the Scalp Microbiome Becomes Unbalanced?

When the balance between beneficial bacteria and naturally occurring yeast is disrupted, the scalp may become more prone to:

  • Excess oil
  • Dryness
  • Itching
  • Flaking
  • Scalp irritation
  • Product buildup
  • Unpleasant scalp odour
  • Increased sensitivity

A healthy scalp depends on maintaining this delicate balance rather than eliminating microorganisms altogether.

What Can Disrupt the Scalp Microbiome?

Several everyday factors may affect your scalp's natural balance.

Overwashing

Cleansing too aggressively or too frequently may strip away the scalp's natural oils and disturb its protective barrier.

Infrequent Washing

Allowing excess oil, sweat, and buildup to accumulate may encourage certain microorganisms to multiply excessively.

Heavy Product Buildup

Styling products, dry shampoo, and excess oils may create an unhealthy scalp environment if not removed properly.

Stress

Stress can influence oil production and skin health, which may indirectly affect the scalp microbiome.

Diet and Lifestyle

Sleep, hydration, nutrition, and overall health all contribute to maintaining healthy skin—including your scalp.

How to Support a Healthy Scalp Microbiome

The healthiest scalp isn't the one with the fewest microorganisms.

It's the one with the best balance.

To support your scalp microbiome:

  • Cleanse your scalp regularly.
  • Use products suitable for your scalp type.
  • Avoid harsh cleansing that strips away natural oils.
  • Remove product buildup regularly.
  • Dry your scalp after washing.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle.
  • Seek professional scalp care when needed.

Healthy scalp care is about balance—not over-cleansing.
