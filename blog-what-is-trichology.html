What is trichology?

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

If you’ve ever searched for answers to problems like hair thinning, dandruff, an oily scalp, or persistent itchiness, you may have come across the word trichology.

But what exactly is trichology, and why is it becoming an important part of professional hair and scalp care?

At Dreamin’ Head Spa & Sculpture, we believe that beautiful hair begins with a healthy scalp. That’s why our treatments are inspired not only by authentic Japanese Head Spa techniques but also by a trichology-informed approach to understanding the hair and scalp.

What Is Trichology?

Trichology is the study of the hair and scalp.

It focuses on understanding how the hair grows, how the scalp functions, and the many factors that can influence hair and scalp health.

A trichology-informed approach considers things such as:

  • Scalp condition
  • Hair density and texture
  • Oil production
  • Dryness or dehydration
  • Product build-up
  • Lifestyle habits
  • Stress
  • Nutrition
  • Hair-care routine

Rather than looking only at the hair itself, trichology recognises that healthy-looking hair is closely connected to the condition of the scalp.

Why Is Scalp Health So Important?

Many people spend time choosing shampoos, conditioners, and styling products, yet give very little attention to the scalp.

The scalp is living skin.

Just like the skin on your face, it requires regular cleansing, balance, and care.

When the scalp becomes overloaded with excess oil, styling products, dead skin cells, or environmental pollutants, it may begin to feel uncomfortable or affect the way the hair looks and feels.

Keeping the scalp clean and balanced helps create a healthier environment for the hair.

Trichology Is More Than Hair Loss

One common misunderstanding is that trichology is only about hair loss.

In reality, it covers a wide range of concerns, including:

  • Dry scalp
  • Oily scalp
  • Itchy scalp
  • Flaking
  • Sensitive scalp
  • Product build-up
  • Hair breakage
  • Hair thinning
  • Changes in hair texture
  • Everyday scalp maintenance

Not every scalp concern is a medical condition, but understanding what you’re experiencing is the first step towards choosing the right care.

What Can a Trichology-Informed Consultation Tell You?

At Dreamin’, every treatment begins with understanding your individual scalp and hair.

During your consultation, we consider factors such as:

  • Your scalp condition
  • Your hair-care routine
  • Colour or chemical services
  • Lifestyle and daily habits
  • Your treatment goals

This allows us to personalise your Head Spa experience instead of using the same routine for every client.

What Trichology Cannot Do

It is important to understand that trichology is not the same as medical diagnosis.

A Head Spa or scalp consultation cannot diagnose medical conditions or replace advice from your GP or dermatologist.

If you have persistent inflammation, open wounds, sudden hair loss, severe itching, or a diagnosed scalp disorder, you should seek advice from an appropriate healthcare professional.

Our role is to provide professional scalp care, educate clients about healthy scalp practices, and personalise treatments based on the visible condition of the hair and scalp.

How Dreamin’ Combines Japanese Techniques with Trichology

What makes Dreamin’ different is the combination of two important philosophies.

The first is authentic Japanese shampoo and Head Spa techniques, where comfort, precision, and hospitality are at the centre of every treatment.

The second is a trichology-informed approach that recognises every scalp is different.

Rather than offering the same treatment to everyone, we tailor our service according to your scalp condition, hair type, and lifestyle.

Our goal is not simply to provide relaxation, but to help you build healthier scalp-care habits that support healthy-looking hair over time.

Healthy Hair Starts with a Healthy Scalp

There is no single product or treatment that works for everyone.

Healthy hair is influenced by many factors, including genetics, hormones, nutrition, lifestyle, and scalp condition.

While a Head Spa is not a medical treatment, regular professional scalp care can help maintain a clean, balanced, and comfortable scalp environment.

Combined with appropriate home care and healthy daily habits, it becomes part of a long-term approach to looking after your hair.

At Dreamin’ Head Spa & Sculpture, we believe education is just as important as treatment.

Every appointment is an opportunity to better understand your scalp, learn about your hair, and receive personalised recommendations that suit your individual needs.

Whether your goal is relaxation, healthier-looking hair, or simply understanding your scalp better, we’re here to help you begin with the right foundation.

Because healthy hair always starts with a healthy scalp.
