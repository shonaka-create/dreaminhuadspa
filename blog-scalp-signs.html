Hair & Scalp Tips

What your scalp is telling you

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 4 min read

Healthy hair is grown, not applied. If the hair is misbehaving, the scalp usually knows why — and it has been trying to tell you for a while.

These are the three signals I see most often in the chair. None of them are unusual, and none of them are hopeless. What they mostly have in common is that people treat the hair, when the answer was one layer down.

1. An itchy or flaking scalp

What people assume: dandruff, so they reach for a stronger, more stripping shampoo.

What I usually find: a scalp that is dehydrated, not dirty. Harsh cleansing strips the scalp's protective barrier; the scalp reacts by drying out and flaking; the flaking looks like dandruff, so out comes the stronger shampoo again. It is a loop, and it tightens.

What actually helps: cleansing gently but properly — lifting the build-up that sits at the root without stripping the skin — and then putting moisture back. That balance is the entire logic of a wet head spa: warm water, thorough cleansing, then nutrients and moisture delivered back into the scalp.

2. Roots that turn oily within a day

What people assume: "I have oily hair, so I should wash more often."

What I usually find: over-washing, ironically. Strip the scalp too aggressively and it compensates by producing more oil, faster. Meanwhile, product and sebum build up around the follicle where a normal wash never quite reaches, so the roots feel greasy while the ends stay dry.

What actually helps: a deep cleanse that reaches the root itself, done occasionally and done properly, rather than daily washing that only ever treats the surface. Most guests notice their hair stays fresh noticeably longer after a head spa — usually the first sign that the balance is coming back.

3. Hair that has gone flat and lost its shine

What people assume: the hair is damaged, or it's just age.

What I usually find: a tight, undernourished scalp. Stress, screen posture, poor circulation — the scalp becomes taut, blood flow to the follicle drops, and hair grows in weaker and lies flatter. You can feel this yourself: try to move the skin on top of your head with your fingertips. If it barely shifts, that is tension.

What actually helps: massage that reaches the muscle and fascia, not just the surface — this is where the Japanese head spa and the traditions of Indian head massage overlap. Loosen the scalp, restore circulation, and volume and shine tend to follow, because the hair is being grown under better conditions.

Three things you can do at home

  • Wash with fingertips, never nails. You are cleansing skin, not scrubbing a pan. Small circular movements, no scratching.
  • Rinse for twice as long as you think you need to. Most people leave product residue behind at the roots, and residue is build-up.
  • Dry your scalp, not just your hair. A damp scalp overnight is an invitation for irritation. Get the roots properly dry.
No two scalps are the same, so no two treatments should be. What your scalp needs today may not be what it needed six months ago. Takashi

When it's worth having it looked at properly

If the itch, the oiliness or the flatness has been going on for months and nothing you have changed at home has shifted it, it is worth having someone actually look at the scalp — with a trichology-informed eye, and ideally a camera — rather than guessing from the hair.

That is where every session at Dreamin' starts: a consultation and scalp diagnosis, before any product touches your head. Then the treatment is chosen for what we can see, not from a routine.

If any of the three above sounded like your own scalp, mention it when you book. We will make time for it.
