Scalp & Hair Health

Understanding dandruff

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

Have you ever noticed white flakes on your shoulders or experienced an itchy, irritated scalp?

Many people assume dandruff is simply caused by a dry scalp or poor hygiene, but that's actually a common misconception.

At Dreamin’, we believe that understanding what dandruff really is and why it develops is the first step toward achieving a healthier scalp and healthier hair.

Let's explore the causes of dandruff, how it affects your scalp, and what you can do to restore balance.

What Is Dandruff?

Dandruff is a common scalp condition where dead skin cells shed from the scalp more rapidly than normal.

Everyone naturally sheds skin cells, but when the scalp becomes irritated or its natural balance is disrupted, those skin cells begin to clump together and become visible as flakes.

Dandruff is not always caused by dryness. In fact, many people with dandruff actually have an oily scalp. A combination of excess oil, inflammation, and an overgrowth of naturally occurring yeast on the scalp is often involved.

Signs You May Have Dandruff

Common signs include:

  • White or yellow flakes on the scalp or clothing
  • Itchy scalp
  • Redness or irritation
  • Oily or greasy scalp
  • Dry, tight feeling on the scalp
  • Scalp sensitivity
  • Mild scalp inflammation

Symptoms may become worse during periods of stress, seasonal weather changes, or when excess product buildup is present.

What Causes Dandruff?

There isn't just one cause of dandruff. Several factors can contribute to its development.

Excess Oil Production

An oily scalp creates an environment where naturally occurring microorganisms can thrive, leading to irritation and visible flakes.

Scalp Microbiome Imbalance

Your scalp is home to millions of beneficial microorganisms.

When this natural balance is disrupted, irritation and flaking can occur.

Product Buildup

Shampoo residue, styling products, excess oil, and dead skin cells can accumulate on the scalp, contributing to irritation and visible flakes.

Dry or Sensitive Scalp

Some people develop flaking because the scalp lacks sufficient moisture or has a weakened skin barrier.

Stress and Lifestyle

Stress, poor sleep, diet, and hormonal changes may all influence scalp health and worsen dandruff symptoms.

How Does Dandruff Affect Your Scalp?

Although dandruff itself doesn't usually cause permanent hair loss, an unhealthy scalp can affect the environment where healthy hair grows.

Untreated dandruff may contribute to:

  • Persistent itching
  • Scalp inflammation
  • Redness
  • Increased oil production
  • Product buildup
  • Blocked hair follicles
  • Scalp discomfort
  • Hair looking dull or lifeless

Frequent scratching may also damage the scalp surface and increase irritation.

Keeping the scalp balanced and comfortable supports healthier-looking hair over time.

Is Dandruff the Same as a Dry Scalp?

Not necessarily.

Although both conditions can produce flakes, they have different causes.

Dry Scalp

  • Small, dry white flakes
  • Tight feeling
  • Lack of moisture
  • Usually improves with hydration

Dandruff

  • Larger white or yellow flakes
  • Often accompanied by excess oil
  • Itching and irritation
  • Caused by scalp imbalance rather than dryness alone

Understanding the difference is important because each condition requires a different approach.

How to Care for a Scalp with Dandruff

Cleanse Your Scalp Regularly

Keeping the scalp clean helps remove excess oil, dead skin cells, and product buildup before they accumulate.

Avoid Scratching

Scratching may temporarily relieve itching but can increase inflammation and damage the scalp barrier.

Choose Gentle Scalp Care Products

Products that support the scalp's natural moisture barrier while gently cleansing can help restore comfort without over-drying.

Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle

Managing stress, staying hydrated, eating a balanced diet, and getting enough sleep all contribute to healthier skin—including your scalp.

Professional Scalp Care Can Help

Sometimes dandruff continues despite using over-the-counter products.

A professional scalp consultation can help determine whether flakes are caused by dandruff, dryness, product buildup, or another scalp condition.
