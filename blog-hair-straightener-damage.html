Scalp & Hair Health

What a straightener really does

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 6 min read

A hair straightener can create sleek, smooth hair in just a few minutes—but have you ever wondered what's actually happening to your hair between those hot plates?

Every time you use a straightener or curling iron, your hair is exposed to intense heat that changes its structure.

At Dreamin’, we believe that understanding how heat affects your hair helps you make better styling choices and reduce long-term damage.

Let's take a closer look.

What Happens When Hair Is Clamped Between a Hair Straightener?

Your hair is made primarily of keratin, a strong protein that gives it its shape and strength.

When the hot plates of a straightener clamp your hair, several things happen almost instantly:

  • Moisture inside the hair begins to evaporate.
  • Hydrogen bonds within the hair temporarily break.
  • The hair is reshaped into a straight form.
  • As the hair cools, new hydrogen bonds form, helping the hair keep its new shape.

This is why your hair stays straight until it becomes wet or exposed to humidity.

What Happens to the Cuticle?

The cuticle is the hair's outer protective layer.

Healthy cuticles lie flat, reflecting light and locking moisture inside the hair.

Repeated exposure to high temperatures can cause the cuticle to:

  • Lift
  • Crack
  • Become rough
  • Lose moisture
  • Wear away over time

Once the cuticle becomes damaged, the inner cortex is exposed, making the hair much more vulnerable to dryness, breakage, and split ends.

What Happens Inside the Hair?

Heat doesn't only affect the surface.

Inside the cortex:

  • Moisture decreases.
  • Keratin proteins become stressed.
  • Hair becomes less elastic.
  • The fibre becomes weaker.

Repeated exposure to excessive heat gradually reduces the hair's strength and flexibility.

How Hot Is Too Hot?

This is one of the most common questions.

While every hair type is different, many hair professionals recommend avoiding unnecessarily high temperatures.

As a general guide:

TemperatureRecommended For
120–150°C (250–300°F)Fine, fragile, or damaged hair
150–180°C (300–355°F)Most healthy hair
180–200°C (355–390°F)Thick or coarse hair (used with caution)
Above 200°C (390°F+)Increases the risk of significant heat damage with repeated use

Higher temperatures don't always produce better results.

In many cases, using a lower temperature with slower, controlled passes is both safer and more effective than using the highest heat setting.

Is 230°C Too Hot?

Many hair straighteners can reach 230°C (450°F).

While this may be useful for some professional salon techniques, using this temperature every day at home can significantly increase the risk of:

  • Dryness
  • Cuticle damage
  • Split ends
  • Hair breakage
  • Colour fading
  • Loss of shine

Unless your hair is extremely coarse and resistant, most people don't need the highest temperature available.

How to Reduce Heat Damage

Healthy styling habits can dramatically reduce long-term damage.

Always Use a Heat Protectant

Heat protectants help reduce moisture loss and create a protective layer around the hair during styling.

Choose the Lowest Effective Temperature

More heat doesn't always mean better styling.

Use the lowest temperature that achieves your desired result.

Avoid Multiple Passes

Repeatedly running the straightener over the same section exposes the hair to unnecessary heat.

Work with small, neat sections so one or two passes are usually enough.

Never Straighten Wet Hair

Hair is at its weakest when wet.

Using a straightener on damp hair can cause rapid water expansion inside the hair shaft, leading to severe structural damage and breakage.

Always ensure your hair is completely dry before using heated styling tools.

Healthy Hair Starts with a Healthy Scalp

Although straighteners affect the hair shaft rather than the scalp, protecting your existing hair is just as important as maintaining healthy new growth.

A balanced scalp combined with proper heat protection creates the best foundation for strong, healthy hair.
