Head Spa Guide

What actually happens in ninety minutes

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 5 min read

People hesitate to book a first head spa for a reason nobody says out loud: they do not know what they are agreeing to. Ninety minutes is a long time to spend somewhere you cannot picture. So let me remove the mystery entirely.

Here is your appointment, start to finish.

Before you arrive

There is nothing to do. Do not wash your hair specially — in fact, please do not. I would rather see your scalp as it actually lives, not as it looks after an hour of preparation. Come as you are, from work or from the beach; it makes no difference to me and it gives me better information.

Arrive five minutes early. The room will already be set for you, and you are the only guest in it.

Minutes 0–15 · Consultation and scalp diagnosis

We sit down and talk first. What your hair does that annoys you. Whether the scalp itches, flakes, tightens, or turns oily faster than it used to. What you have already tried. Whether anything has changed — a move, a new job, a stressful year, a shampoo you switched to and never questioned.

Then I look at the scalp itself, informed by trichology — the study of hair and scalp health. This is the part most people have never had done, and it is the part they talk about afterwards. An oily scalp and a dehydrated one can look identical from a metre away and need almost opposite treatment. I want to know which one I am dealing with before I open a single bottle.

By the end of these fifteen minutes you will know something about your own head that you did not know when you walked in. That happens every time, and it is free either way.

Minutes 15–70 · The head spa

You lie back on a completely flat shampoo stand, imported from Japan. This is worth one sentence of explanation, because it is the reason people sleep here: on a standard salon basin your neck is bent back over a rim, and your body braces against it for the entire treatment. On a flat stand there is nothing to brace against. The full story of that stand is here.

Then, in order:

  • Warm water, and a lot of it. Longer than you expect. Most people have never had their scalp properly wet through.
  • Layered cleansing. Lifting the build-up that sits around the follicle and that a normal wash never reaches. Chosen for the condition I found, not from a routine.
  • Treatment. Nutrients and moisture delivered back into a scalp that is now clean enough to take them.
  • Massage. Japanese head spa technique layered with the traditions of Indian head massage — reaching the muscle and fascia over the skull, not just the skin. Scalp, neck, shoulders.

You will not be making conversation during this. You do not have to. Most guests are asleep within the first fifteen minutes, and I take that as the review that matters.

Minutes 70–90 · The blow-dry, and why it is not optional

You do not leave here with wet hair. Ever.

In Japan the finish is part of the treatment, not something you are upsold at the end, and I brought that rule with me. Every head spa at Dreamin’ ends with a full professional blow-dry, included in the price — and a blow-dry is a $110 service on its own menu line, so this is not a small inclusion.

You walk out styled, in daylight, ready for wherever you are going next. For a lot of guests this is the moment the whole ninety minutes suddenly makes sense.

On the way out · What you take with you

You will get a straight answer about what I found, what caused it, and what to do at home about it. If your shampoo is working against you, I will say so. If nothing is wrong and you simply carry your stress in your head, I will say that too, and I will tell you how often to come back — usually every four to six weeks, sometimes not at all.

The scalp diagnosis is the part guests do not expect and never forget. Everything else you can feel. That one, you learn. Takashi

The questions people are too polite to ask

Do I have to talk? No. Say so at the start and I will work in silence.

Can I come on a work day? Yes — you leave blow-dried, which is precisely the point. A lot of our guests come between commitments.

Is it awkward if I fall asleep? It is the goal. Snoring included; I have heard it all.

What if my scalp is in bad condition and I am embarrassed? Then you are exactly who this treatment is for, and I promise you I have seen worse this week. Mention it when you book and I will allow extra time in the consultation.

That is the whole ninety minutes. Nothing about it is mysterious — it is just longer, quieter and more deliberate than anything you have had done to your head before.
