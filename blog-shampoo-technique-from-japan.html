Our Philosophy

The shampoo technique I learned in Japan

By Takashi Iwata Owner & Head Spa Practitioner 10 min read

Many people think shampooing is simple.

Apply shampoo, create some lather, wash the hair, and rinse.

Before receiving professional training in Japan, I may not have fully understood just how much knowledge, precision, and care could be contained within something called a shampoo service.

While training at a salon in Japan, I experienced a genuine shock.

It wasn’t because of one special movement or one unusual product.

It was because every single detail had a purpose.

From the moment the client was escorted to the shampoo area until the final rinse was completed, nothing was treated casually.

That experience completely changed the way I think about shampooing—and it became one of the foundations of Dreamin’ Japanese Head Spa.

The Service Begins Before the Shampoo

A professional shampoo service doesn’t begin when water touches the hair.

It begins with the way the client is escorted to the shampoo station.

How should we guide them?

What should we explain before they sit or lie down?

Is their neck comfortable?

Are their clothes protected?

Do they understand what will happen next?

Are they warm enough?

These details may seem small, but together they create trust and comfort.

In Japan, service is not only about completing a task correctly. It is about considering how the guest feels throughout the entire experience.

This reflects the Japanese spirit of Omotenashi—thoughtful hospitality expressed through attention, preparation, and genuine care.

Even the Water Temperature Matters

The first water that touches the scalp can immediately influence the client’s comfort.

Water that is too hot may feel harsh and may unnecessarily dry or irritate the scalp. Water that is too cool may feel uncomfortable and may not support effective cleansing.

A trained shampoo professional checks and adjusts the temperature carefully.

But it is not only about choosing one temperature and leaving it unchanged.

The temperature may need to be considered throughout the service, depending on the client’s comfort, scalp condition, treatment stage, and surrounding environment.

Something as simple as warm water becomes part of the overall experience.

Pre-Rinsing Is Not Just Wetting the Hair

One of the biggest lessons I learned was that pre-rinsing has a clear purpose.

We are not simply making the hair wet before applying shampoo.

We are thinking about what needs to be cleansed.

The water should move through the scalp and hair in a deliberate order, helping loosen and rinse away:

  • Sweat
  • Excess sebum
  • Dust
  • Loose skin cells
  • Styling product residue
  • Environmental impurities

The direction of the water, the angle of the shower head, the length of the rinse, and the areas receiving attention all matter.

A thorough pre-rinse prepares the scalp for shampoo and allows the cleansing process to be more effective.

Water Pressure Is Part of the Technique

Water pressure is not something that should be ignored.

Different areas of the scalp may require different positioning and control.

The water needs enough pressure to rinse effectively, but it should never feel aggressive or uncomfortable.

The pressure, distance, angle, and movement of the water are adjusted with purpose.

The goal is not simply to remove shampoo quickly.

The goal is to rinse thoroughly while maintaining comfort from beginning to end.

Creating the Right Lather

Shampoo should not simply be poured onto one area of the scalp and rubbed around.

Creating the right lather is part of professional technique.

The amount of shampoo, the amount of water, how the product is distributed, and how the foam is created all influence the quality of the cleanse.

A well-prepared lather helps shampoo spread more evenly across the scalp and reduces unnecessary friction on the hair.

The foam should support the technique—not replace it.

There Is a Correct Order

Professional shampooing follows a deliberate sequence.

The scalp is divided into areas, and each area is cleansed carefully so that nothing is missed.

The hairline, temples, crown, sides, behind the ears, and back of the scalp all require attention.

Following an organised sequence helps create consistency and ensures that the entire scalp is properly cleansed.

Without a system, it is easy to repeatedly wash one area while neglecting another.

Which Fingers You Use Matters

Professional shampooing is performed with the fingertips—not the nails.

But even that explanation is too simple.

The fingers used, their positioning, how the hand supports the head, and how the movement travels across the scalp all affect the experience.

The hands must remain controlled but flexible.

The technique should feel secure without becoming harsh.

The goal is not to scratch the scalp or simply create strong stimulation.

It is to cleanse the scalp carefully while creating rhythm, comfort, and flow.

Pressure Must Be Controlled

More pressure does not automatically mean a better shampoo.

Some clients prefer firmer stimulation, while others have sensitive scalps or areas that require gentler care.

Pressure needs to be adjusted according to:

  • Scalp condition
  • Client comfort
  • Hair density
  • Areas of tension
  • Sensitivity
  • The purpose of each movement

A professional shampoo service requires awareness.

We must constantly pay attention to how the scalp responds and how the client feels.

The Entire Hand Has a Role

Japanese shampoo technique is not performed by moving only the fingertips.

The fingers, palms, wrists, arms, posture, and body positioning all work together.

Good body mechanics allow the practitioner to maintain consistent rhythm and pressure without creating unnecessary tension.

The client should feel continuous, natural movement rather than disconnected or rushed actions.

When the hands move correctly, shampooing becomes more than cleansing.

It becomes a carefully structured sensory experience.

“You’re Only Washing Someone’s Head”—Are You?

Some people may think:

“It’s only shampoo. You’re just washing someone’s head.”

But after learning professional shampoo technique in Japan, I believe it is much more than that.

There is a proper shampoo service.

There is knowledge behind it.

There is technique behind it.

There is hospitality behind it.

There is a difference between simply making the hair clean and providing a shampoo experience that makes someone feel completely cared for.

Being able to relax while another person carefully washes your hair and scalp can be one of the most comforting and satisfying human experiences.

You do not need to concentrate.

You do not need to make decisions.

You can close your eyes, release control, and allow someone to care for you.

That feeling is difficult to recreate when washing your own hair at home.

Shampoo Is Not a Small Part of the Service

In many salons, shampoo may be treated as a quick step before the main appointment.

At Dreamin’, we see it differently.

Shampooing is not something we rush through to reach the next service.

It is a professional service in its own right.

It is an opportunity to:

  • Cleanse the scalp thoroughly
  • Observe the condition of the scalp
  • Reduce unnecessary buildup
  • Support ongoing scalp maintenance
  • Protect the hair from excessive friction
  • Help the client feel safe and comfortable
  • Create a moment of genuine rest

Every rinse, every movement, and every touch should have a reason.

Bringing What I Learned in Japan to Dreamin’

The training I received in Japan changed my professional standards.

It taught me that quality is not created by one impressive technique.

Quality is created by performing every small step properly.

The escort.

The explanation.

The water temperature.

The pre-rinse.

The water pressure.

The lather.

The washing sequence.

The fingers.

The pressure.

The hands.

The final rinse.

All of it matters.

At Dreamin’, I want to share this level of care with every guest.

Our approach is not about copying a few movements and calling them Japanese.

It is about respecting the discipline, thoughtfulness, and attention to detail behind Japanese salon service.

Come and Experience a True Shampoo Service

A proper shampoo service is difficult to explain completely through words.

It needs to be experienced.

You need to feel the difference between hair simply being washed and being cared for through professional technique.

At Dreamin’ Japanese Head Spa, we want you to experience shampooing as it was taught to me in Japan—with precision, comfort, purpose, and genuine hospitality.

Come and allow yourself to receive a service that many people overlook, but that can become one of the most satisfying parts of your entire salon experience.

Because shampoo is not just preparation for something else.

When it is performed properly, shampoo itself is the service.
